Grandma's Buttermilk Biscuits

Ingredients:
Ingredient Amount
Active dry yeast 1 pkg
Warm water 1 tbsp
Self-rising flour (White Lily is best) 5+ cups
Crisco shortening 1 cup
Buttermilk 2 cups

Steps:

  1. Put ~1 tbsp warm water into a 2 cup measuring cup and sprinkle 1 package of active dry yeast over the top. Set aside.
  2. Pre-heat oven to 500°F
  3. Cut 5 cups of self-rising flour with 1 cup of Crisco shortening into pea-sized clumps
  4. Check yeast. It should be bubbly across the top. Use a fork to mix the yeast thorougly with the water.
  5. Measure two cups of buttermilk into the 2 cup measuring cup (with the yeast).
  6. Add buttermilk/yeast mixture to the flour and crisco and mix well with a large spoon, adding flour in small increments as necessary.
  7. Once dough forms ball that pulls away easily from the bowl (not sticky), dust a breadboard or clean counter with flour and place dough in center.
  8. Knead dough with hands 3 or 4 times.
  9. Use a rolling pin to roll dough into a large circle about 3/4 inch thick.
  10. Use a biscuit cutter or glass to cut biscuits and place them about 1/2 inch apart on a non-stick, ungreased cookie sheet or pan.
  11. Knead the dough remnents back into a ball and repeat rolling, cutting, and kneading until all biscuits are made.
  12. Bake in oven for about 10-12 minutes or until golden on top.

Macaroni and Cheese

Ingredients:
Ingredient Amount
Garlic clove, small, chopped 1 clove
Pipette pasta (or your choice of small pasta) 1 lb
All-purpose flour 1/4 cup
Cayenne, ground 1/2 tsp
Kosher salt
Breadcrumbs 2 cups
Butter, unsalted 7 tbsp
Fontina cheese, grated 5 oz
Gruyere cheese, grated 5 oz
Parmigiano reggiano, grated 18 tbsp
Whole milk 3 1/2 cups

Steps:

  1. Prepare pasta according to packaging, cooking until al dente, about 1 minute short of package instructions
  2. Make the topping
    1. Melt 3 tbsp butter
    2. Stir in chopped garlic
    3. Add breadcrumbs
    4. Add 2 tbsp Parmigiano reggiano
    5. Toss with hands to mix
    6. Set aside
  3. Heat oven to 350°F
  4. Melt remaining butter in 3 or 4 quart saucepan on low heat
  5. Whisk in flour and continue until fully incorporated into butter (about 3 minutes), should gently bubble and froth without coloring
  6. Add milk in small amounts, stirring or whisking continually, making sure to smooth all lumps
  7. Once all milk is added, continue to heat and whisk or stir until sauce is smooth and has the consistency of heavy cream
  8. Raise heat to medium until the sauce simmers
  9. Cover and allow sauce to simmer about 10 minutes, whisking occasionally
  10. Turn off heat and add cheeses and cayenne
  11. Season with salt
  12. Pour pasta into 9x13-inch baking dish
  13. Pour sauce over pasta and stir
  14. Sprinkle topping evenly over pasta
  15. Bake for about 15 minutes, until top is golden brown

Spanakopita Bites

Ingredients:
Ingredients Amount
Vegetable oil 3 tbsp
Small onion, chopped 1
Green onion, chopped 3
Frozen spinach, thawed and squeezed dry 10 oz
Feta cheese, crumbled 4-6 oz
Cottage cheese OR ricotta cheese OR cream cheese 1 cup
Egg 1
Salt and pepper to taste
Phyllo, thawed 1 lb
Butter, melted 8 tbsp

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F
  2. Sauté white and green onions in 1 tbsp vegetable oil until soft, 3 or 4 minutes
  3. Mix spinich, feta, cottage cheese, and egg in medium bowl
  4. Add onions to bowl and salt and pepper to taste
  5. Combine remaining butter and oil
  6. Carefully remove phyllo dough from package and stack
  7. Cut the phyllo dough into 2x2 inch quares
  8. Use a pastry brush to brush each well of a mini muffin pan
  9. Place 1 or 2 squares of phyllo dough into each well, pushing down to create a cup
  10. Brush phyllo dough in the cups with butter and oil
  11. Add more squares of phyllo dough in 1 or 2 per layer, brushing between each layer until desired thickness (usually 3 layers)
  12. Place a heaping teaspoon of filling into each phyllo cup, filling to the top
  13. Bake at 375°F for 15 to 20 minutes
